How to Play

Race for place, not life total. Build a deck, dodge like a maniac, and cross the line first.

Objective

The winner is the player with the highest place when the race ends. place is where you are on the track. Cards move you forward or shove others back.

  • Everyone starts at 0.
  • Card effects will say things like “Gain 8 places” or “Target loses 6 places”.
  • Whoever reaches the finish line first, wins!.

Setup

  1. Each player chooses 1 Driver and 1 Kart.
  2. Build a deck of 20 cards (Items and Utilities).
  3. Then add 20 fuel to your deck.
  4. Shuffle your deck and draw 7 cards.
  5. Everyone starts at place 0.

Drivers

Drivers are your character card. They have a unique ability or once-per-turn trick.

  • Only one Driver per player.
  • Some Drivers swap positions, copy abilities, or bend dodge rules.

Example Driver

Karts

Karts define how your racer handles.

  • Speed – how fast you can move up the track.
  • Handling – how good you are at dodging attacks.
  • All Karts have a passive ability (“When you dodge…”, “When you play a Utility…” etc.).

Example Kart

Turn Structure

  1. Start: Resolve any “at the start of your turn” effects.
  2. Draw: Draw 1 card.
  3. Draw: Place 1 fuel card.
  4. Race: Roll your movement for this turn.
  5. Main: Play Items and Utilities, activate abilities.
  6. End: Check any passive abilities, discard down to 7 cards if you have more than that.

First player is chosen by rolling the highest number. Then it goes clockwise.

Card Types

Items

Items are the chaos. Shells, bombs, dodgy mushrooms… all the good stuff.

  • They usually have 2 abilities
  • Some of these abilities attack other player(s).
  • Other abilities help you dodge or move places!

Example Item

Utilities

Utilities are one-shot tactical plays. They don’t require hit rolls.

  • Draw extra cards, recycle your discard, or spike someone’s place.
  • Often say things like “For the rest of this turn…” or “This round…”.
  • These are special cards that do lots of cool things.

Example Utility

places & Movement

Most effects either gain you places or make someone else lose it.

  • If a card says “gain 10 places”, move yourself forward 10.
  • If it says “target driver loses 6 places”, they move backwards 6.
  • Some effects care whether a driver is ahead or behind you.

If an effect would take someone below 0, they just stay at 0. No negative track... yet.

Hits, Dodges & Handling

  • Most Item abilities have a Hit value (like “Hit 17+”).
  • Target Driver must Roll the die: if their roll + handling beats the Hit, they dodge the attack.
  • Some cards add flat bonuses to hit/dodge, or say “unblockable” (no dodging).

FAQ (Quick Rulings)

Can I play more than one card in a turn?

Yes, as long as you can pay all costs and nothing on the table says you can’t.

What if two things trigger at the same time?

The current player chooses the order their effects go on the stack. Then the next player, and so on.

What if a card doesn’t say when it ends?

Unless it clearly says “this round” or “this turn”, treat it as a one-off effect that resolves and is done.
